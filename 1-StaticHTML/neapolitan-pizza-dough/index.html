Neapolitan Pizza Dough

A classic Neapolitan pizza dough that uses high-quality ingredients and a long fermentation period to develop a soft, delicious crust when baked at high temperatures.

Yields: 6 crusts

Prep: 15 minutes Wait: 2 days

Ingredients

1000 grams Caputo "00" flour
650 grams water (85-90°F)
20 grams fine sea salt
1/4 teaspoon active dry yeast

Directions

  1. Dissolve the yeast in the warm water and let sit for five minutes.
  2. Add the water to the flour in a stand mixer bowl and combine roughly by hand.
  3. Using a spiral dough hook, mix the flour and water in the stand mixer for five to ten minutes, gradually adding the salt as the dough homogenizes.
  4. When the dough is mixed, remove it from the bowl and shape it into a large ball. Drizzle olive oil over the dough ball and rub it gently over the surface.
  5. Place the dough ball in a 12 quart clear plastic tub with a lid and let rise at room temperature for twelve hours or until tripled in size.
  6. Gently remove the dough from the plastic tub onto a clean working surface, taking care not to compress the dough. Using a dough knife, cut the dough into six equal-sized pieces.
  7. Shape each piece into a tight ball. Coat the dough balls with flour and place in a plastic bag or airtight container. Let rise in the refrigerator for 24 to 48 hours.
  8. Remove the dough from the refrigerator two hours before baking.
  9. To shape the crust, generously flour both sides of a dough ball and place on a floured wooden pizza peel. Taking care not to disturb the outer edge or overstretch the center of the dough, gently stretch the dough apart with both hands, then rotate and repeat.
